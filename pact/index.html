Abstract

Photoacoustic computed tomography (PACT) is an emerging technology that combines the benefits of optical and ultrasound imaging, with advantages including deep penetration, high imaging speed, and superior image quality. Recently, three-dimensional PACT (3D PACT) systems have been developed, which is capable of producing high-resolution, multipurpose 3D images for applications ranging from brain to breast imaging. However, high-quality 3D reconstruction is time-consuming, requiring the target to remain still. Additionally, 3D PACT system has high manufacturing cost due a much higher amount of sensors compared to its 2D counterpart. We propose accelerating 3D PACT acquisition and reconstruction using physics-aware neural operators (Pano), which is a deep learning architecture agnostic to the number of sensors used in the system, making it versatile for different sparse configurations of the sensor. In addition, Pano respects the 3D geometric arrangement and alignment of the sensor and target locations. It is also physics-aware, which uses the forward imaging model to minimize hallucinations of the deep learning model. We demonstrate the robustness and efficacy of Pano in reconstructing high-quality images from real phantom data, achieving consistent performance even when the number of transducers is reduced or with limited-angle acquisition. This advancement holds promise for making 3D-PACT more accessible and practical for both preclincal and clinical use.
